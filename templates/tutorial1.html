Tutorial 1: Basic Newspaper Layout

In this tutorial, we will cover the basics of newspaper layout, including:

  • The masthead
  • The front page
  • The inside pages
  • The back page

We will also provide tips on how to create a visually appealing and easy-to-read newspaper.

The Masthead

The masthead is the name of the newspaper, usually displayed at the top of the front page. It typically includes the newspaper's logo, its motto, and its contact information.

The Front Page

The front page is the most important page of the newspaper. It is where the most important stories are placed. The front page should be visually appealing and easy to read.

The Inside Pages

The inside pages of the newspaper are where the rest of the stories are placed. These pages are typically divided into sections, such as news, sports, and business.

The Back Page

The back page of the newspaper is often used for less important stories, such as obituaries and classified ads.

Tips for Creating a Visually Appealing and Easy-to-Read Newspaper

  • Use a consistent font throughout the newspaper.
  • Use headlines to draw attention to important stories.
  • Use images to break up the text and make the newspaper more visually appealing.
  • Use white space to make the newspaper easier to read.
  • Proofread the newspaper carefully before printing.
